### Post 1: r/UKPersonalFinance

**Title:** "Making Tax Digital starts April 2026 — here's what sole traders actually need to do"

**Body:**
With MTD for Income Tax launching on 6 April 2026, I've been looking into what's actually required. Here's a summary for anyone else trying to figure this out:

**Who's affected first:**
- Sole traders and landlords with income over £50,000 (from April 2026)
- Income over £30,000 follows in April 2027
- Income over £20,000 follows in April 2028

**What you need to do:**
1. Keep digital records of all business income and expenses
2. Submit quarterly summaries to HMRC (not annual — quarterly)
3. Use MTD-compatible software (HMRC doesn't provide any — you need third-party)
4. Have your UTR and Government Gateway account ready

**The quarterly schedule:**
- Q1: 6 Apr – 5 Jul (due by 7 Aug)
- Q2: 6 Jul – 5 Oct (due by 7 Nov)
- Q3: 6 Oct – 5 Jan (due by 7 Feb)
- Q4: 6 Jan – 5 Apr (due by 7 May)

I built a free tool to help check your readiness and start tracking expenses in the right format — happy to share if anyone's interested. Main thing is don't leave it to the last minute, the deadline is only 2 months away.

Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/making-tax-digital-for-income-tax

**Body:**
Just a heads up for anyone who hasn't started thinking about MTD yet — HMRC have confirmed they will NOT provide free software for quarterly submissions. You'll need to buy third-party tools.

Current options range from:
- FreeAgent: ~£24/month (£288/year)
