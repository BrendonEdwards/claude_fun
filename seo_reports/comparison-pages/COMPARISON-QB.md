### MTD Compliance: How Each Tool Works

Both tools help you stay Making Tax Digital compliant — but they work differently.

**QuickBooks Sole Trader:**
QuickBooks is on HMRC's recognised software list and submits quarterly updates directly to HMRC through a built-in integration. You connect your bank account, categorise transactions, and QuickBooks sends the quarterly update to HMRC on your behalf. It also supports your annual Self Assessment tax return.

**QuarterlyUK:**
QuarterlyUK tracks your income and expenses across 14 HMRC-aligned categories and generates quarterly summaries ready for submission. However, the submission itself happens via CSV export to an HMRC-approved bridging software — QuarterlyUK does not submit directly to HMRC (yet — this is on the product roadmap).

**What this means practically:**

If you want a single tool that does everything including the submission itself, QuickBooks has the edge here.

If you are comfortable using QuarterlyUK for record-keeping and a bridging tool for the actual submission step, the workflow works well — and costs significantly less.

Direct HMRC submission is confirmed as a planned feature on the QuarterlyUK roadmap.

### Features: Side by Side

#### Expense Tracking

Both tools track expenses using categories aligned to HMRC's self-employment requirements.

QuarterlyUK uses HMRC's 14 expense categories directly. QuickBooks uses its own categorisation system with a mapping to HMRC requirements.

**Verdict: Draw** — both cover HMRC compliance. QuarterlyUK's direct HMRC category alignment makes the mapping transparent.

#### Invoice Generation

Both tools generate professional invoices. QuickBooks includes automatic invoice reminders and status tracking. QuarterlyUK's invoices are simple but professional, with VAT support.

**Verdict: QuickBooks** — richer invoicing with reminders and more customisation.

#### Mileage Tracking

QuickBooks includes mileage tracking. QuarterlyUK does not (it is on the roadmap).

**Verdict: QuickBooks**

#### Mobile App

QuickBooks has dedicated iOS and Android apps. QuarterlyUK is browser-based — it works on mobile browsers but has no native app.

**Verdict: QuickBooks**

#### Bank Feeds

QuickBooks connects directly to your bank account and imports transactions automatically. QuarterlyUK does not have bank feed integration — you add expenses manually or via CSV import.

**Verdict: QuickBooks**

#### Data Privacy

This is where QuarterlyUK has a significant and unique advantage.

QuarterlyUK stores all your financial data locally in your browser — nothing is sent to any external server. Your income, expenses, and invoice data never leave your device.

QuickBooks stores your data on Intuit's cloud servers. This is standard practice for cloud software, but it means a third party holds your financial records.

For sole traders who handle sensitive client invoicing or who simply prefer their financial data to stay private, QuarterlyUK's local storage model is a genuine differentiator.

**Verdict: QuarterlyUK** (for privacy-conscious users)

#### Self Assessment Support

QuickBooks Sole Trader is designed to support your Self Assessment tax return preparation. QuarterlyUK does not currently include Self Assessment filing tools.

**Verdict: QuickBooks**

#### Price Predictability

QuarterlyUK: £2.50/month, fixed, with a 30-day notice commitment before any change.

QuickBooks: promotional pricing that rises significantly after year one.

**Verdict: QuarterlyUK**

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I switch from QuickBooks to QuarterlyUK?**
Yes. Export your financial data from QuickBooks as a CSV, then set up QuarterlyUK from scratch. Since QuarterlyUK stores data locally, there is no data migration — you simply begin tracking from the date you switch.

**Does QuarterlyUK work with the same HMRC bridging software as QuickBooks exports?**
QuarterlyUK exports a CSV that works with HMRC-approved bridging software. The specific bridging software tools that are compatible with QuarterlyUK's CSV format are listed on the [QuarterlyUK FAQ page](https://quarterlyuk.com).

**Is QuickBooks MTD-ready for April 2026?**
Yes. QuickBooks Sole Trader is HMRC-recognised and supports MTD for Income Tax.

**Is QuarterlyUK MTD-ready for April 2026?**
QuarterlyUK prepares your quarterly summaries in HMRC-compliant format. You submit them via bridging software. It is MTD-compatible but not directly HMRC-recognised in the same category as QuickBooks.

Because your data is stored locally in your browser, it stays there after you cancel. You can also export your data at any time.
